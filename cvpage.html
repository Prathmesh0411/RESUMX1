1. What is a CV (Curriculum Vitae)?

CV (short for Curriculum Vitae) is the Latin word for “course of life.” In a nutshell, a CV is a summary of who you are as a professional, including your work history, education, and skills. Done right, a CV is supposed to “sell you” to potential employers and land you a job.

2. What’s the Difference Between a CV and a Resume?

Technically speaking, a CV and a resume are the same document. The only difference is that Americans call it a "resume," while Europeans and others refer to it as a CV. A European CV can be between two and three pages long, depending on experience. A US resume is usually one or two pages max. For more on the CV vs Resume article. topic, check out our article.
